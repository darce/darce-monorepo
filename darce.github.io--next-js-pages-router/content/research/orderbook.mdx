## What is an Order Book?

In a marketplace, some market participantswant to **buy**, and others want to **sell**. An order book is a list that organizes all these intentions in one place.

Every financial market uses an order book to match buyers with sellers.

## Key Concepts to Notice

### The Spread

Look at the gap between the **best bid** (highest buy offer) and the **best ask** (lowest sell offer). This gap is called the **spread**.

- A **small spread** means buyers and sellers mostly agree on priceâ€”the market is "liquid"
- A **large spread** means there's more disagreementâ€”it costs more to trade immediately

### Order Imbalance

The **order imbalance** tells you whether there's more buying or selling pressure:

- **Positive imbalance** (more bids than asks): More buyers are waitingâ€”prices might rise
- **Negative imbalance** (more asks than bids): More sellers are waitingâ€”prices might fall

### Market Depth

The colored bars show **how much** people want to buy or sell at each price level. Longer bars mean more volume. This is called "depth"â€”it shows how much the price would move if a large order came in.

## How Trades Happen

A trade occurs when a buyer's bid **matches** a seller's ask:

1. **Market order**: *"I want to buy right now at whatever price is available"* â€” This instantly takes the best ask
2. **Limit order**: *"I want to buy, but only at $103,000 or less"* â€” This sits in the order book waiting

When someone places a market buy order, they "lift" the lowest ask. When someone market sells, they "hit" the highest bid. The order book updates instantly.
